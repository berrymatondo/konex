Recommendation rationale

The proposed allocation preserves the mandate order of safety, liquidity and return. No change improves expected return at the expense of a higher-priority objective; return is optimized only within the freedom left after both are met.

Safety

Credit quality is held broadly constant, with a modest rotation out of corporate bonds into supranational issuers. Average rating stays at AA+ and the share rated AA− or higher rises slightly, keeping capital preservation the binding first objective.

Liquidity

Treasury-bill weight is raised to lift the liquidity-coverage ratio ahead of an elevated near-term intervention buffer. The standing emergency floor and forecast outflows remain fully covered under the base scenario and under the liquidity-crisis stress.

Return & diversification

Within those bounds, a small increase in gold improves diversification against inflation and reserve-currency risk, while trimming cash captures additional carry. Duration is left essentially unchanged, avoiding added interest-rate risk for a marginal yield gain.

Governance · version controlled

Policy & limits

Investment guidelines and risk limits set by the Central Bank. Each set is versioned: edits create a draft, drafts are submitted and approved, and the active version feeds every optimization run.

Approved versions are read-only

To change a threshold on an approved set, create a new version. It stays a draft until it is submitted and approved, then it becomes the active set.

Governance · reusable templates

Assumption management

Reusable sets of optimization assumptions — expected returns, inflation, FX and rate outlook. A run selects one template; drafts can be reviewed before they are relied on.

Governance · reusable stress scenarios

Scenario management

Define the market and liquidity conditions a run is tested against — rate, spread and FX shocks plus an outflow uplift. Approved scenarios appear as selectable options on the optimization form.
